THE PROJECT

Spectra is the 4th iteration of the Murdoch Open Showcase, an annual Murdoch University student-organised event exhibiting works created by creative media students. Like a white light beam travelling through a prism and turning into a rainbow, Spectra represents a student’s university journey from connecting with other students to being exposed to different experiences, resulting in expanding the spectrum of who they are when this phase of their lives ends.

I worked with three other people on the showcase’s online spaces. My primary responsibility was developing the front end of the website.

ENCOURAGING A DEEPER UNDERSTANDING OF THE ARTS

The arts have often been undervalued and this was evident with the amount of funding we received in comparison to previous years. People who aren’t involved in the industry don’t have a clear understanding of how specialised creative media is and the amount of work and effort that creatives put into their projects.

To change people’s perception of creative fields, we created and implemented a content strategy for the website that not only provided students a platform to share their work but to also educate people on what students do and learn at university that transforms them into creatives.

Increasing need for events to have a virtual space...

BUILDING ON PAST SHOWCASES

Each year the showcase has been held, its dedicated online spaces kept growing bigger. For Spectra, we wanted to continue building and improving on what previous showcases has introduced for its online spaces.

There also has been an increasing need for events to have a virtual space, especially given the impact the pandemic has had on live events and exhibitions. But based on the previous years the showcase has run, we also found that the showcase’s online spaces received minimal engagement. We worked with the other teams to promote the online spaces and integrate it into the physical event.

Photos of research we presented at our stakeholder presentation | Photography by Fioline Oetomo

UNDERSTANDING THROUGH RESEARCH

As a cohort, we conducted interviews, surveys and empathy mapping to determine the needs of current students and staff for the showcase as well as attendees. We discovered that students and staff wanted the showcase to be a platform where students can gain exposure for their work. The public were interested in interacting with people who made works shown at the showcase.

From these insights, the online spaces team decided we wanted to show process and behind the scenes of students’ works in the showcase’s online spaces.

SEEING WHAT'S POSSIBLE

We also conducted analysis of online spaces from similar industries and a visual investigation to help narrow our scope for the showcase’s online spaces. We looked at interactive spaces as it fit with the Spectra theme of being impacted by engaging experiences. And following in the footsteps of the 2020 team, we also looked at immersive virtual spaces.

CREATING A PIECE OF A DESIGN SYSTEM

When designing the website, we had to make sure that it fit with the overall theme and the brand identity that the branding team came up. We went through multiple iterations of wireframes but we managed to achieve a cohesive look by keeping the layout simple and staying in constant communication with the branding team.

We decided to treat the website as an informative platform rather than a portfolio...

KNOWING OUR LIMITS

While we wanted the showcase to be immersive and accessible online, we had to be realistic about what we could achieve as a team of four in under three months.

We decided to focus purely on the website and treat it as an informative platform about each creative media discipline rather than a portfolio for individual student pieces to maximise our workflow. We also decided to produce video content about Spectra and the work that went into creating this showcase. These videos would feature on the website and shared on social media.

GETTING THE JOB DONE

The actual development of the website was fairly streamlined and wasn’t too difficult as we delegated tasks amongst the four of us based on our strengths. I was most comfortable with coding in HTML, CSS and JavaScript in our team which was why I ended up being the front-end developer. But I also assisted with the WordPress side of website as well.

EVALUATING THE PROJECT

It’s hard to say for certain on website’s performance. Ideally, it would have been great to set up a web analytics tool to have access to data of the website’s traffic but our main priority was building the website which I’m personally very proud of considering the constraints we were working under.

The showcase itself was a huge success despite our lack of financial support. 600 attendees visited the showcase, an increase from 2020. Because of this turnout, the 2022 team managed secure significant financial support from Murdoch University.
