THE PROJECT

The Fremantle Arts Centre (FAC) is home to a variety of arts courses, events and exhibitions as well as a rich heritage dating as far back as the 1860s.

The Concert Series is a hypothetical service offering that focuses solely on FAC’s live music events in an effort to encourage music enthusiasts and locals to support the live music scene in Fremantle.

OPTIMISING THE CONCERT EXPERIENCE

The FAC’s most popular offerings are its live music events. For their target audience mostly interested in live music, the FAC website is currently not suited for finding out the upcoming performing artists as the website also promotes art exhibitions, courses and other non-music events.

A dedicated online space focused on FAC’s live music events was developed to optimise people’s, particularly FAC’s music-loving target audience, concert experience before they even attend a FAC concert in person.

UNDERSTANDING THE AUDIENCE

I conducted design sprints to fully understand the target audience’s needs. This involved creating personas of FAC’s target audience, empathy mapping, journey mapping and point of view and how might we statements.

The design sprints helped identify the need to highlight FAC’s unique selling point, which is its heritage, and making information about events and featured artists streamlined to engage and cater to the target audience.

CREATING A DISTINCT LOOK

The main challenge of this project was incorporating the history and heritage of FAC whilst still being engaging to attract music enthusiasts and locals.

I decided to play around with different logo designs to help define the visual direction of this project as logos are a great way of succinctly communicating a project’s identity.

The main challenge was capturing both Fremantle's heritage and the energy of live music events...

GETTING THE AUDIENCE'S ATTENTION

The content layout of the home page was informed by my design sprints where audiences have expressed events and event information as the most important information they actively seek out.

I also made sure to factor in the organisation’s own goals and added information and call to actions to encourage audiences to further engage with FAC.

I managed to combine the conflicting historical and contemporary aesthetics by mimicking shapes commonly found in Fremantle’s historical architecture and using bright colours against a dark background to replicate concert lighting.

ENSURING USABILITY

After coding the website, I conducted user testing to make sure that the website was functional for users and communicated its connection to live music and Fremantle’s heritage to audiences.

From this testing, several changes were made, like adding visual cues and change the size of elements, to lessen the difficulty of certain website tasks.

EVALUATING THE PROJECT

This project could help FAC get more engagement by collaborating with local musicians and community groups in Fremantle and Western Australia. People’s experience with FAC’s live music events could also be further optimised by implementing findings from this project into different spaces like social media.
